Decision/Problem_1 In horticulture: Is it commercially viable to grow grapes for wine production in the ACT? If so what weather factors do farmers have to keep an eye on?

Knowledge grapes flower in spring, producing their crop throughout summer[How to grow and prune grapevines - bunnings Australia (no date) Bunnings Australia. Available at: https://www.bunnings.com.au/diy-advice/garden/planting-and-growing/how-to-grow-and-care-for-grapevines (Accessed: 13 October 2023).] The overall optimum temperature grapevine growth is 77 and 90 degrees F (25 to 32C). Any temperature below this optimum range causes vegetative growth to become limited. Warmer temperatures in general tend to produce more consistent grape harvests. Lack of rainfall can be a severe influence on grape productivity in the absence of good quality irrigation water. Johnson and Robinson (2001) recommend a minimum level of rainfall/irrigation of 20 inches (500 mm), higher if the growing season is characterized by high evapotranspiration rates. Excess rainfall is also a problem and most quality wines are produced in regions where annual rainfall does not exceed 28 to 32 inches (700 to 800 mm) (Jackson et al., 1987). Wind - Light breezes maintain air circulation around the berries stopping the buildup of humidity and maintaining an even temperature within the canopy. However, high winds can cause serious damage to grapevines, especially to vines in the spring and early summer, when shoots are tender and more easily broken. [Vineyard site selection (no date) Grape Growers Handbook: A Guide To Viticulture for Wine Production. Available at: https://www.wine-grape-growing.com/wine_grape_growing/vineyard_site_selection/vineyard_site_selection_climatic_components.htm (Accessed: 13 October 2023).]

Information Needed Average temperature throughout spring and Summer, average yearly rainfall, average wind speed seasonally.

spring_summer_data <- act_weather_data %>%
  filter(Month %in% c("9", "10", "11", "12", "1", "2")) %>%  # Spring and Summer months
  group_by(Month) %>%
  summarize(
    Avg_Temperature = mean((`3pm_Temperature`)),
    Avg_Rainfall = mean(`Rainfall_(mm)`),
    Avg_Wind_Speed = mean((`9am_wind_speed_(km/h)` + `3pm_wind_speed_(km/h)`) / 2)
  )

ggplot(spring_summer_data, aes(x = Month)) +
  geom_line(aes(y = Avg_Temperature, color = "Average Temperature"), size = 1) +
  geom_line(aes(y = Avg_Rainfall, color = "Rainfall"), size = 1) +
  geom_line(aes(y = Avg_Wind_Speed, color = "Wind Speed"), size = 1) +
  labs(
    x = "Month",
    y = "Temp (C), Wind (km/h), Rain (mm)",
  ) +
  scale_x_discrete(limits = month_order) +
  scale_color_manual(values = c("Average Temperature" = "blue", "Rainfall" = "green", "Wind Speed" = "purple")) +
  theme_minimal()

Decision/Problem_2 In horticulture: Can rice be grown commercially in the ACT? If so when is the best season for planting?

Knowledge Originally from tropical regions, rice is a warm-season crop that requires constant heat and humidity to grow. It can be cultivated as an annual in warm climates, though, even if you dont reside in the tropics. During the growing season, the best temperature for rice is between 70F and 99F (21C and 37C). It is possible to grow it only where nighttime lows stay at 60F (15C) or higher for a minimum of three months annually. How come its so important? Germination is temperature-dependent, so the lows for the growing crop must always be above that mark. The water needs of growing rice far exceed those of any other crop. Thus, only in locations with a minimum annual rainfall of 45 inches (115 cm) does rice growing take place. The best growing conditions can be found in areas where yearly rainfall is between 69 and 118 inches (175 and 300 cm) on average. Paddies must be inundated for at least 10 weeks during the growing season, with water depths ranging from 1 inch (25 mm) at the transplanting stage to as much as 6 inches (150 mm) throughout the crop life cycle. [Kogut, P. (2023) How to grow rice: Planting, care, & harvesting practices, EOS Data Analytics. Available at: https://eos.com/blog/how-to-grow-rice/ (Accessed: 14 October 2023). ] Humidity should range somewhere from 60 - 85%. [De Silva, R. and Dayawansa, N.D.K. (2016) Assessment of the suitability of temperature and relative humidity for rice cultivation in rainfed lowland paddy fields in Kurunegala district, Research Gate. Available at: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/319227475_Assessment_of_the_suitability_of_temperature_and_relative_humidity_for_rice_cultivation_in_rainfed_lowland_paddy_fields_in_Kurunegala_district (Accessed: 13 October 2023).]
